Sadhana साधन

The Science of Spiritual Transformation. An ontological commitment to mastering the self and dissolving the ego.

०१ Ontological Foundation

Sadhana represents the conscious application of the human will against the mechanical inertia of the physical and mental bodies. It is not a peripheral choice, but a radical shift from being a passive recipient to an active architect of inner reality.

Primary Objective

"Chitta Vritti Nirodha"—The cessation of the fluctuations of the mind.

Viveka

Discernment between the transient and the eternal.

Vairagya

Dispassion toward temporary sensory results.

Mumukshutva

Unquenchable thirst for ultimate liberation.

मन

The Mechanics of the Mind

Samskaras & Vasanas

Every thought leaves an imprint (Samskara). Repetitive imprints form latent tendencies (Vasanas). Sadhana creates "Sadhana-Samskaras" to neutralize destructive patterns, effectively rewiring the psychic structure.

Abhyasa: The Tech of Repetition

Practice must be long-term (Dirgha Kala), uninterrupted (Nairantarya), and performed with total sincerity (Satkara) to become firmly grounded.

The Four-Fold Path

Path of Heart

Bhakti Yoga

Alchemy of emotion. Sublimating human feeling into divine devotion. Loss of "I" in the "Thou".

Path of Action

Karma Yoga

Selfless service. Acting with 100% intensity but 0% attachment to results (Phala).

Path of Mind

Raja Yoga

Scientific mastery through the eight limbs (Ashtanga). Focusing the mind to reach Samadhi.

Path of Inquiry

Jnana Yoga

Using Viveka (discernment). The process of "Neti-Neti" (not this, not that) to find the Atman.

Brahmamuhurta

"The Hour of Brahman"

The 90 minutes before sunrise is dominated by 'Sattva'. Free from psychic noise, this is the optimal window to activate the 'Sushumna Nadi'.

Mauna

"Noble Silence"

Vak-Sadhana involves conserving 'Prana' by disciplining speech. Energy lost in gossip is redirected inward to fuel spiritual vitality (Ojas).

The Antarayas (Obstacles)

Vyadhi (Illness) Styana (Dullness) Samshaya (Doubt) Alasya (Laziness)
Samadhi & Sahaja

The highest state is Sahaja Avastha—where the sage returns to the world, anchored in the infinite. Sadhana is no longer something you 'do', but something you 'are'.
